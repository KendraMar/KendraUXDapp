# Dispute Resolution

This document describes how conflicts and disputes are resolved in the Apollo project.

## Philosophy

Disputes are normal in collaborative projects. Our goal is to:
- Resolve conflicts constructively
- Maintain relationships
- Learn from disagreements
- Move forward together

## Types of Disputes

| Type | Examples | Primary Resolution |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| **Technical** | Architecture, implementation | Maintainer + ADR |
| **Process** | How something should be done | Discussion + consensus |
| **Interpersonal** | Conduct, communication | Mediation |
| **Governance** | Rule interpretation, authority | Council |
| **Law Violation** | Alleged rule breaking | Defined escalation |

## Resolution Principles

### 1. Assume Good Faith

Start from the assumption that the other party:
- Is acting in the project's interest
- May have information you don't
- Isn't intentionally causing harm

### 2. Prefer Direct Resolution

- Talk directly to the person first
- Keep scope small initially
- Escalate only when needed

### 3. Focus on Interests, Not Positions

- Understand *why* someone wants something
- Find solutions that address underlying needs
- Avoid winner/loser framing

### 4. Document for Clarity

- Write down agreements
- Reduce ambiguity
- Create precedent for future

## Resolution Process

### Level 1: Direct Discussion

**Who:** The parties involved  
**When:** First attempt for any dispute  
**Duration:** 1-7 days

**Steps:**
1. Reach out to the other party
2. Explain your perspective
3. Listen to their perspective
4. Seek common ground
5. Agree on resolution

**Success:** Issue resolved, relationship maintained

### Level 2: Facilitated Discussion

**Who:** Parties + neutral facilitator  
**When:** Direct discussion unsuccessful  
**Duration:** 1-2 weeks

**Steps:**
1. Request a facilitator (any uninvolved maintainer)
2. Facilitator arranges discussion
3. Each party explains their view
4. Facilitator helps identify options
5. Parties agree on path forward

**Facilitator Role:**
- Neutral, not decision-maker
- Ensures both parties are heard
- Suggests process, not outcome
- Documents agreement

### Level 3: Maintainer Decision

**Who:** Relevant maintainer(s)  
**When:** Facilitation unsuccessful, or technical dispute  
**Duration:** 1 week

**Steps:**
1. Escalate to relevant maintainer
2. Each party submits their position (written)
3. Maintainer may ask questions
4. Maintainer makes decision
5. Decision is documented

**Maintainer Authority:**
- Binding for technical decisions in scope
- Can set direction, end debate
- Should explain reasoning

### Level 4: Council Review

**Who:** Stewardship Council  
**When:** Maintainer decision disputed, or governance matter  
**Duration:** 2-4 weeks

**Steps:**
1. File formal request with Council
2. Council reviews materials
3. May request additional input
4. Council deliberates
5. Council issues decision

**Council Authority:**
- Final for governance matters
- Binding on all parties
- Can override maintainer decisions
- Interprets Constitution and laws

### Level 5: Community Vote

**Who:** Active Contributors  
**When:** Council recommends, or constitutional matter  
**Duration:** 3-4 weeks

**Steps:**
1. Council frames the question
2. Positions are documented
3. Voting period opens
4. Votes are counted
5. Majority/supermajority decides

## Specific Dispute Types

### Technical Disagreements

**Scenario:** Two approaches to implementation

**Resolution Path:**
1. Discuss tradeoffs in PR/issue
2. If stuck, create ADR with options
3. Maintainer decides if no consensus
4. Document the decision and rationale

### Code Review Conflicts

**Scenario:** Disagreement about review feedback

**Resolution Path:**
1. Discuss in PR comments
2. If stuck, request another reviewer
3. If still stuck, maintainer decides
4. Area maintainer has final say in scope

### Conduct Concerns

**Scenario:** Someone's behavior is problematic

**Resolution Path:**
1. Speak directly if safe to do so
2. Otherwise, report to maintainer or Council
3. Confidential investigation
4. Appropriate action taken
5. Follow Code of Conduct enforcement

### Governance Disputes

**Scenario:** Disagreement about rule interpretation

**Resolution Path:**
1. Discuss in governance channels
2. Request Council clarification
3. Council issues interpretation
4. If major, may require law/amendment

### Role Disputes

**Scenario:** Disagreement about someone's authority

**Resolution Path:**
1. Review role documentation
2. Discuss with role holder
3. Escalate to their oversight level
4. Council decides if unresolved

## Formal Complaint Process

For serious matters requiring formal handling:

### Step 1: File Complaint

Submit in writing to Council (or designated ombudsperson):
- Nature of the complaint
- Parties involved
- Relevant evidence
- Desired resolution

### Step 2: Acknowledgment

Within 48 hours:
- Complaint received
- Expected timeline
- Interim measures if needed

### Step 3: Investigation

Within 2 weeks:
- Gather information
- Interview parties
- Review evidence
- Maintain confidentiality

### Step 4: Decision

Within 1 week of investigation:
- Written decision
- Rationale
- Required actions
- Appeal options

### Step 5: Appeal

If dissatisfied:
- 7 days to appeal
- Appeal to full Council (if not already involved)
- Or community vote for constitutional matters

## Enforcement Actions

For violations of laws or conduct standards:

| Severity | Examples | Possible Actions |
|----------|----------|------------------|
| Minor | First-time minor violation | Warning, coaching |
| Moderate | Pattern of minor, or moderate violation | Temporary restriction |
| Serious | Major violation, harm caused | Suspension, ban |
| Severe | Egregious or repeated serious | Permanent ban |

**Process:**
1. Alleged violation reported
2. Investigation as appropriate
3. Opportunity to respond
4. Decision and action
5. Documentation
6. Appeal available

## Confidentiality

- Disputes are handled as privately as appropriate
- Conduct matters are confidential
- Final decisions may be summarized publicly
- Personal details are protected

## Recusal

Decision-makers must recuse if:
- Directly involved in the dispute
- Personal relationship with a party
- Financial interest in outcome
- Stated bias

Recused individuals:
- Do not participate in decision
- May provide information if asked
- Should not lobby others

## Mediation Resources

For complex disputes, external mediation may be arranged:
- Council can engage neutral mediator
- Costs borne by project if available
- Parties must agree to process

## Prevention

Better than resolution:
- Clear expectations
- Open communication
- Regular check-ins
- Early intervention
- Healthy conflict culture
